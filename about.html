ABOUT ME

Tell my story

I'm Jayani Malsha, a final-year BSc (Hons) Computer Science undergraduate at the Informatics Institute of Technology, affiliated with the University of Westminster (UK), graduating in September 2026. Over a one-year machine learning internship, I designed and deployed AI solutions across healthcare, cybersecurity, forecasting, and chatbot domains, taking projects from raw data through to trained models and live web interfaces.

My career objective is to grow into a machine learning engineer or AI-focused software developer who builds systems that are both technically rigorous and genuinely useful in production, not just accurate in a notebook.

I'm most passionate about the point where deep learning meets real software: turning a model into something people can actually rely on, whether that's an API, a dashboard, or a full-stack application. I enjoy the full pipeline, from data preprocessing and model training to backend architecture and deployment.

My areas of interest span deep learning architectures (CNNs, RNNs, LSTMs, transformers), natural language processing and large language models, time-series forecasting, explainable AI, and backend engineering with Node.js, NestJS, and FastAPI.
